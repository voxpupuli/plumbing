# Romain Tarière (@smortex)

Hi!

I would like to nominate myself for the next PMC election.

If you do not know me yet, you can read [my application to the 2023 election](https://github.com/voxpupuli/plumbing/blob/master/share/elections/2023-01/smortex.md), which is still up-to-date regarding my background.

What has changed since then is the context around Puppet.
Since Perforce acquired Puppet and closed its source code, the community has had to adapt.
One major step was forking Puppet and creating the OpenVox project to provide the Free/Libre alternative the community wants.

At the same time, collaboration with Perforce has become increasingly difficult.
Pull-requests on many puppetlabs-\* modules remain unreviewed and unmerged.
For example, several of these modules still lack support for current operating systems, despite fixes proposed by Vox Pupuli contributors months or even years ago.
I believe many practitioners have experienced this gradual decline in the workflows they rely on.

Important work has been made to increase our independence from upstream, but we are not there yet.
While I understand why we refrained from breaking relationships with Perforce, we need to recognize that we have postponed several key steps toward independence for too long.
This hesitation is now negatively impacting both the project and the community.

I believe it is time for us to acknowledge the role we already play.
In practice, Vox Pupuli / OpenVox is now the reference implementation / ecosystem around Puppet, and we are the main point of contact for contributors.
We should embrace this position and build on it e.g. by providing quality reference documentation that can be quickly updated / fixed by our community, or by becoming a module distribution point that collect and provide metrics usable by modules maintainers to make decisions.

I think I refrained from opening my mouth about this for too long, and maybe it is time for me to share my strong feelings about the current state of affairs and the direction we should take as part of the PMC ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯.
